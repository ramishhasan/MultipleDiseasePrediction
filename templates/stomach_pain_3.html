STOMACH PAIN MEDICATION INFORMATION




AGE (Adults)

Age group 17+ Years


What To Do

Antacids: These are used for mild to moderate stomach pain caused by acid reflux or indigestion. Examples include Tums, Rolaids, and Maalox.

Proton Pump Inhibitors (PPIs): These are used for more severe acid reflux symptoms. Examples include Nexium, Prilosec, and Prevacid.

H2 Blockers: These are also used for acid reflux and include medications such as Pepcid and Zantac.

Nonsteroidal Anti-Inflammatory Drugs (NSAIDs): These are used for pain relief, but can also cause stomach irritation and increase the risk of ulcers. Examples include ibuprofen (Advil, Motrin) and naproxen (Aleve).

Acetaminophen: This is a pain reliever that is less likely to cause stomach irritation than NSAIDs. Examples include Tylenol.




It is important to note that these medications should only be used as directed and under the guidance of a healthcare provider. Dosage and frequency of medication can vary based on age, weight, and other health factors.
