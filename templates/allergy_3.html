ALLERGY MEDICATION INFORMATION



AGE (Teens and Adults)

Age group of 12 and older


What To Do

Antihistamines: Many of the same antihistamines that are used for children can also be used for teens and adults.

Nasal corticosteroids: These are still a good option for this age group.

Decongestants: These can help relieve congestion and are often available in both oral and nasal spray forms. Examples include pseudoephedrine and oxymetazoline.

Immunotherapy: This is a treatment that involves gradually exposing the person to increasing amounts of the allergen to build up their immune system's tolerance. It's typically reserved for people with severe allergies that aren't well-controlled with other medications.



It's important to note that you should always talk to a doctor or pharmacist before starting any new medication, especially if you have any underlying health conditions or are taking other medications.
